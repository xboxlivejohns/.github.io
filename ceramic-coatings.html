Ceramic Coatings — long-life, hydrophobic protection

Every ceramic package includes at least a single-stage machine polish so the coating bonds to a clean, glossy surface. If your paint has heavier swirling we’ll recommend multi-stage correction first.

Think of ceramic as the protective shell that locks in the finish created by machine polishing. Together they deliver faster washing, stronger chemical & UV resistance, and deeper gloss that holds up to daily use.

Why choose a ceramic coating?

Easier cleaning

The slick, hydrophobic surface repels dirt and water. Grime doesn’t cling, so safe washes take less time and effort.

Stronger protection

Improved resistance to chemicals, road film and UV fading compared to traditional waxes and sealants.

Longer-lasting gloss

Coatings last years, not months. Pair with correction for that wet, mirror-like finish that holds up to daily use.

Ceramic coatings & machine polishing — how they work together

Machine polishing (paint correction) chases out the swirls and haze that dull the finish. The ceramic coating then seals in that clarity, adds UV and chemical resistance, and makes future washes safer. If you’re unsure how much polishing you need, read our machine polishing guide or book a lighting inspection.

Preparation & application — what we do

  1. Assessment & lighting check — we inspect paint and discuss goals.
  2. Decontamination — safe wash, iron fallout and tar removal, clay if required.
  3. Paint correction (recommended) — single or multi-stage polishing to perfect the finish.
  4. Panel wipe & coating — apply coating in controlled sections; cure per manufacturer spec.
  5. Final check & advice — we inspect under lighting and provide aftercare guidance.

Maintenance & aftercare

  • Use a pH-neutral shampoo and safe wash method (two buckets, grit guards, quality microfibres).
  • Avoid harsh roadside chemicals and automatic brush washes.
  • For coated cars, use the recommended topper every few months to maintain slickness and beading.
  • Remove bird droppings and tree sap quickly to protect the coating.

Quick FAQs

Is a coating better than wax?

Yes — coatings are tougher and last years. Waxes look great but need frequent re-application.

Do I need correction first?

Strongly recommended. Coatings lock in the finish — correcting first ensures you’re sealing a flawless surface.

How long before I can wash?

Avoid washing for ~7 days to allow full cure. Gentle maintenance after that keeps performance high.
