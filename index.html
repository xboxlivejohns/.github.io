Paint Correction — reclaim gloss, remove defects

Paint correction is the process of removing or reducing defects in the clear coat to restore clarity, gloss and depth. We use multi-stage machine polishing and measure paint thickness to work safely.

What it includes

  • Full vehicle assessment & paint depth check
  • Decontamination: iron fallout & tar removal
  • Multi-stage machine polishing (single → multi-stage)
  • Finishing polish and surface prep for protection

Typical times

Small cars: 2–4 hours (single stage). Medium / large / multi-stage: 4–8+ hours depending on condition and stages required.

Pricing guide

From £150 (single-stage, small cars). Multi-stage correction and premium vehicles quoted after inspection. Exact price depends on severity of defects and vehicle size.

Ceramic Coatings — long-life hydrophobic protection

A ceramic coating bonds with the paint to form a durable, glassy layer that is highly hydrophobic and chemically resistant. Coatings are best applied after paint correction for maximum gloss and longevity.

Benefits

  • Superior water beading & easier cleaning
  • UV protection and increased chemical resistance
  • Longer-lasting gloss vs traditional wax

Packages

Express (1 year) · Professional (3 year) · Premium (5+ year). We match coating to vehicle use and budget.

Pricing guide

From £195 for express packages. Full correction + premium coating typically from £450+. Quotes provided after inspection.

Paint Protection Film (PPF)

PPF offers invisible, high-impact protection for bumpers, sills, mirrors and full-wrap options. Modern films often have self-healing topcoats for minor swirl/abrasion resistance.

Who it's for

  • New car owners who want long-term protection
  • Drivers who do a lot of motorway miles
  • Owners who want to protect show/track vehicles

Options

Partial coverage (bumper/sills), full bonnet/wing, or full vehicle wrap. Films vary by finish (gloss/satin/stealth).

Pricing

Quoted on inspection — typical high-impact jobs start from £600 depending on coverage and film chosen.
